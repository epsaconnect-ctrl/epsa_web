Bulk Upload

Upload hundreds of questions at once using either a structured CSV template or a formatted Word/PDF document.

📋 CSV Format Requirements

Your CSV must have the following columns in order:

theme
course
learning_outcome
bloom_level
difficulty
question_text
option_a
option_b
option_c
option_d
correct_idx (0=A, 1=B, 2=C, 3=D)
explanation
⚠ Max 500 questions per batch. Duplicates (same question text) are automatically skipped.
Structured Word / PDF Format

Use a .docx or .pdf when you want to write questions as readable blocks instead of spreadsheet rows.

Theme: Counseling, Health and Clinical Psychology Course: Psychopathology Learning Outcome: Criteria for Defining Abnormal Behaviours Bloom Level: Understanding Difficulty: medium Question: Which symptom profile best matches generalized anxiety disorder? A: ... B: ... C: ... D: ... Correct Answer: B Explanation: ... --- Repeat the same block for the next question.
Use the field labels exactly as shown, separate each question with ---, keep one correct answer per block, and keep the file at or below 8 MB.

Help & Guidelines

Everything you need to know about writing excellent psychology exam questions.

Writing Good Questions
  • Write a single, clear, unambiguous stem
  • Make all four options plausible to test comprehension
  • Avoid double negatives and trick wording
  • Match difficulty to the target Bloom's level
  • Write a detailed explanation for the correct answer
  • Specify why distractors are wrong - learners benefit
Bloom's Taxonomy Guide
Remembering: Recall of facts (define, list, recall)
Understanding: Explain concepts in own words (describe, summarize)
Applying: Use knowledge in new situations (solve, apply)
Analyzing: Break down complex info (compare, contrast)
Evaluating: Make judgments and defend conclusions (critique, justify)
Creating: Synthesize new knowledge (design, construct)
Review Process

Each question you submit enters a pending review queue. EPSA admins will review for content accuracy, language clarity, and Bloom's alignment. Approved questions are added to the live question bank. Rejected questions are returned with admin notes so you can revise and resubmit.

Adaptive Difficulty

Once your questions are used in Mock Exams, the system automatically tracks correctness rates and time-on-question. Questions with >=70% correct rate are reclassified as easy, and those with <=40% as hard. This keeps the exam calibration accurate over time.
