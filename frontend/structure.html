Constitutional framework (reference)

Structure, Statutes, and Governing Bodies of EPSA

This page explains how leadership, representation, and elections are organised in the Ethiopian Psychology Students' Association (EPSA), and how those rules are reflected in the EPSA web platform. It links formal roles (NEB, NEC, NRC) to the actual Python modules and API routes that enforce eligibility, voting, exams, and messaging. Where this page and the registered constitution or bylaws differ, the registered instruments prevail.

Contents: Art. I · Art. II NEB · Art. III NEC · Art. IV NRC · Art. V Elections · Art. VI Platform · Art. VII Interpretation

Article I — Definitions and supremacy of instruments

1.1 "EPSA" means the Ethiopian Psychology Students' Association.

1.2 "Member" means a student admitted to the national platform in accordance with EPSA registration rules. In code, approved members typically have users.status = 'approved' (see applicant approval flows in backend/admin.py).

1.3 The Constitution and duly adopted bylaws are the supreme internal instruments. The digital platform (Flask blueprints under backend/) operationalises membership, voting, exams, clubs, and messaging; it does not replace those instruments.

Article II — National Executive Board (NEB)

Composition and mandate

2.1 The NEB is the highest continuous executive authority between general assemblies: strategy, policy execution, and oversight of national programmes (trainings, certification exams, partnerships).

How the platform supports NEB work

Administrators use the secured admin dashboard (admin/dashboard.html, js/admin.js) which calls REST endpoints under /api/admin/... defined in backend/admin.py: approving students, scheduling trainings, publishing exams, configuring voting phases, and managing clubs and grants.

Key module: backend/admin.py — applicants, trainings, exams (create / publish via is_active), voting phase configuration, executive and NRC tooling.

Responsibilities (operational view)

  • Approve membership and maintain an accurate national roll (admin applicant routes).
  • Publish national exams when ready: exams are created as drafts then toggled active so students see them via GET /api/exams in backend/exams.py.
  • Supervise elections: activate phases, approve nominations, and finalise results so the student portal and NRC workflows stay aligned with policy.

Article III — National Executive Committee (NEC)

Role

3.1 The NEC operationalises NEB decisions: portfolios (programmes, communications, discipline), day-to-day coordination, and execution of assembly resolutions.

Phase 2 executive elections in software

After Phase 1 establishes university-level representation, Phase 2 in backend/voting.py opens national executive nominations. Only students who appear as active, eligible NRC members in the nrc_members table may submit nominations for Phase 2 positions—the server enforces this in nominate() before inserting into nominations.

backend/voting.py → nominate(): Phase 2 requires nrc_members.status='active' AND eligibility_status='eligible'.

Accountability

The NEC remains accountable to the NEB and to the membership; admin tooling in backend/admin.py (executive governance panels in js/admin.js) is used to record decisions, vacancies, and handovers in line with your internal procedures.

Article IV — National Representative Council (NRC)

Representative function

4.1 The NRC channels institutional and geographic perspectives into national deliberation. On the platform, NRC membership is backed by database rows synced from Phase 1 outcomes (see NRC management tooling exposed alongside voting in the admin UI).

Selection (Phase 1 — university-scoped)

Phase 1 candidates are nominations tied to the voter's university. The API filters the ballot in get_candidates() so each student only sees approved nominees from users.university matching their own row.

backend/voting.py → get_candidates(): if phase_number == 1, nominations are restricted with WHERE u.university = voter.university.

Powers (as reflected in code)

  • Phase 1 winners feed representative structures used later for Phase 2 eligibility.
  • Student-facing NRC-related data and documents may be served through backend/students.py (e.g. governance document paths and portal copy)—check that module for the exact endpoints your deployment exposes.

Article V — Elections, voting process, and integrity

Phase 1University ballotPhase 2Executive ballotOne vote / phase

The live voting engine is implemented in backend/voting.py and consumed by the student dashboard (js/dashboard.js) via API.getActiveCandidates, API.nominateSelf, and API.castVote (js/api.js).

1
Active phase

The server selects the current phase with _active_phase(): a row in voting_phases where is_active = 1 and status = 'active'. If none exists, the API returns active: false and an empty candidate list.

voting_bp routes: /voting/candidates, /voting/nominate, /voting/vote, /voting/results
2
Nomination

POST /voting/nominate requires an approved, non-graduated member. Phase 2 additionally requires eligible NRC membership. Files such as manifestos are stored under the uploads folder configured in the Flask app.

3
Casting a vote

POST /voting/vote rejects duplicate ballots (votes uniqueness per voter and phase) and validates that the candidate has an approved nomination in the active phase. In Phase 1, the voter and candidate must share the same university string.

4
Results

GET /voting/results aggregates vote totals for reporting in the portal. Admins finalise phases and sync structures through backend/admin.py (election lifecycle and governance cycle helpers).

Integrity measures encoded today

  • Eligibility: membership status, graduation status, and NRC flags are checked server-side before nominations or votes are accepted.
  • Ballot secrecy: handled by your organisational policy; the database stores an anonymous link between voter_id, candidate_id, and phase_id for auditability.
  • Disputes: procedural resolution remains a human process; the platform preserves machine-readable records for investigation.

Article VI — Platform services (exams, identity, messaging)

Examinations

Admins create exams and questions via backend/admin.py. Publishing sets exams.is_active so backend/exams.py includes them in GET /api/exams. Students must hold a verified face profile (face_embeddings.registration_verified = 1) and pass a recent exam face check before starting a timed attempt.

backend/exams.py — list_exams, verify_exam_face, start_exam (JWT user id normalised to int for reliable joins).

Facial verification

Registration and exam flows use backend/face_verification.py (OpenCV-based detection and embeddings). Face login in backend/auth.py requires both a strong embedding match and a live-vs-enrolled-photo check so behaviour stays consistent with registration. Analysis responses can include a facial_features object (eyes, nose, mouth geometry, teeth-visible heuristic) for UI feedback.

Messaging and student support

Direct messages use backend/messaging.py. Students can reach the primary admin account via GET /messages/support-contact; conversations and replies use /messages/conversations, /messages/<partner_id>, and POST /messages. Admins see the same threads in the "Student Messages" section of the admin dashboard.
